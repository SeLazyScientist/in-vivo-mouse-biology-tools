Rule of thumb

In small-N mouse studies (n=5–10/group), Bayesian methods with weakly informative priors typically give you more honest uncertainty than t-tests. Frequentist tests assume the data tell the whole story; Bayes lets you carry forward what's already known about effect sizes.

Whatever you pick, plot the raw data first.
